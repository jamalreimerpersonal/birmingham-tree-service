## Immediate Safety First

After a storm, prioritize safety before anything else.

### Dangers to Watch For

- Downed power lines (stay away, call utility company)
- Unstable trees or branches
- Debris and hazards
- Gas leaks or structural damage
- Hidden dangers like weakened limbs

## Immediate Actions

### 1. Assess Safety

Walk your property carefully. Look for:
- Fallen trees or large branches
- Branches hanging dangerously
- Power lines down
- Structural damage
- Hazardous debris

### 2. Contact Professionals if Needed

Call an arborist immediately if:
- Power lines are involved
- Trees are near structures
- Large trees are unstable
- You're unsure about safety
- The damage is extensive

### 3. Document Damage

- Take photos for insurance
- Write descriptions of damage
- Note tree locations and size
- Save receipts for cleanup expenses

### 4. Contact Your Insurance

Report tree damage to your insurance company promptly. Many policies cover tree removal from storms.

## What NOT to Do

- **Don't climb damaged trees** – They're unstable
- **Don't use chainsaws** if untrained – Very dangerous
- **Don't touch power lines** – Potentially fatal
- **Don't ignore warning signs** – Dangers can appear later
- **Don't delay removal** – Hazardous trees get worse

## Professional Storm Cleanup

After severe storms, call a certified arborist for:
- Hazard tree assessment
- Safe removal of fallen trees
- Dangerous branch removal
- Property damage restoration
- Debris cleanup

## Insurance Coverage

Many homeowner policies cover:
- Storm-damaged tree removal
- Debris cleanup
- Tree replacement in some cases

Check your policy. Some require specific documentation.

## Recovery Timeline

**Immediate (24-48 hours):** Safety assessment and emergency removal
**Short-term (1-2 weeks):** Major debris cleanup
**Medium-term (1-2 months):** Complete restoration and replanting
**Long-term:** Landscape recovery and new tree planting

## Prevention for Next Time

- Maintain trees during good weather
- Remove weak or diseased branches
- Keep trees healthy and pruned
- Have emergency numbers ready
- Consider tree removal before storm season

The best response to storm damage is quick action and professional help.
