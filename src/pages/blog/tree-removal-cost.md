## Tree Removal Cost Factors

Tree removal pricing varies based on several factors. Understanding these will help you get accurate estimates.

### Tree Size

**Small trees (up to 20 feet):** $200-$600
**Medium trees (20-60 feet):** $600-$1,500
**Large trees (60+ feet):** $1,500-$3,500+

Larger trees require more equipment, manpower, and safety precautions, which increases the cost.

### Tree Condition

**Healthy trees:** Standard removal cost
**Dead/diseased trees:** 10-20% less (easier to remove)
**Hazardous trees:** 20-50% more (increased safety measures)

Dead trees are often easier to remove since they're brittle. Hazardous trees near structures cost more due to specialized equipment and extra care.

### Location & Access

**Easy access:** Standard pricing
**Limited access:** +$200-$500 additional
**Difficult location:** +$500-$1,500+

Trees in tight spaces, near power lines, or close to structures require extra caution and specialized techniques.

### Stump Grinding

**Stump grinding:** $100-$300 per stump
**Full stump removal:** $200-$500 per stump

Most quotes include stump grinding. Full removal (with all roots) costs more.

### Debris Removal

**Included cleanup:** Often standard
**Haul away:** +$200-$500
**Chipping on site:** +$100-$300

Ask if debris removal is included or if it's extra.

## Getting Accurate Quotes

Always get in-person estimates from licensed arborists. Phone quotes can't account for all factors. Most reputable companies offer free estimates.

## Money-Saving Tips

- **Remove multiple trees together:** Most companies offer discounts
- **Clear access:** Remove obstacles to make work easier
- **Off-season removal:** Winter is typically cheaper
- **Chip debris:** Onsite chipping costs less than haul-away

## Why Cost Matters Less Than Quality

Choosing the cheapest option can be risky. You want:
- Licensed and insured professionals
- Proper safety equipment
- Certified arborists
- Written estimates and contracts

A slightly higher price ensures quality work and protects you legally.
