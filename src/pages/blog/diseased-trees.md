## Common Tree Diseases in Birmingham

The Birmingham area's climate creates conditions for various tree diseases. Early detection is key.

## Warning Signs of Disease

### Canopy Problems
- **Thinning crown:** Leaves appear sparse or light
- **Dieback:** Branches dying from tips inward
- **Discolored foliage:** Unusual yellowing or browning
- **Early leaf drop:** Leaves falling before season

### Bark Issues
- **Cankers:** Sunken areas or lesions on bark
- **Peeling bark:** Bark separating from trunk
- **Oozing sap:** Sticky discharge from wounds
- **Visible damage:** Splits, cracks, or damage

### Growth Problems
- **Stunted growth:** Lack of annual growth
- **Weak branches:** Branches breaking easily
- **Poor healing:** Wounds not closing properly
- **Leaning:** Unusual lean or instability

## Common Birmingham Tree Diseases

### Oak Wilt
- Affects oak trees primarily
- Causes rapid wilting and death
- Often fatal if not caught early
- Spreads through root systems

### Anthracnose
- Fungal disease causing leaf spots
- Brown patches on leaves
- Usually managed with trimming
- Can be severe in wet springs

### Dutch Elm Disease
- Affects elm trees
- Blocks water transport
- Usually fatal to the tree
- Requires immediate removal

## When to Remove a Diseased Tree

Consider removal if:
- The disease is untreatable
- Treatment would be extremely expensive
- The tree is declining rapidly
- The disease spreads to other trees
- The tree is hazardous
- It threatens your landscape investment

## Professional Diagnosis

Never guess about tree disease. A certified arborist can:
- Accurately diagnose the problem
- Assess treatment options
- Determine prognosis
- Recommend removal if necessary
- Suggest replacement trees

## Prevention Tips

- Plant disease-resistant varieties
- Maintain tree health through proper care
- Prune diseased branches promptly
- Avoid injuring tree bark
- Water trees during droughts
- Remove dead branches regularly

Early detection and professional care can save valuable trees.
